What is Hannukah

Historically, Hannukah is the celebration of the successful Judean rebellion against the Seleucid Empire in 167 BCE. In 168BCE, King Antiouchus IV Epiphanes banned Jewish practice, likely due to the misinterpretation of conflicts between some Jewish high priests. This campaign was met with resistance from the Maccabee (AKA Hasmonean) family - led by Judah Maccabee. After 7 years of ancient guerilla warfare the revolt was successful and Judea gained independence under the Maccabees.

Hamantashen

Hamantashen are triangular filled pastries. They are traditionally eaten around Purim because the triangular shape resembles the shape of Haman's hat.

Winter Salad

A salad with arugula, cranberries, and whatever we can pick outside. The date of Hannukah changes each year (unless you use the lunar calendar) but it always falls some time in the winter. A couple years ago it was on Thanksgiving

Rugelach

Rugelach is a rolled pastry, traditionally filled with cinnamon. We will have apple butter rugelach. Very tasty

Sufganiyot

Sufganiyot are jelly filled doughnuts - very classic for Hannukah. We will have mini sufganiyot that can be dipped in a variety of jams.
